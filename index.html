Health Information & Guidelines

Ayurvedic Diet Guidelines

  • Eat fresh, seasonal, and locally sourced foods according to your dosha
  • Include six tastes (sweet, sour, salty, pungent, bitter, astringent) in your meals
  • Choose whole grains, fresh vegetables, and fruits appropriate for your constitution
  • Avoid processed foods, excessive sugar, and foods that aggravate your dosha
  • Drink warm water throughout the day, especially upon waking
  • Practice mindful eating in a calm environment
  • Include digestive spices like ginger, cumin, coriander, and turmeric
  • Maintain regular meal timings and avoid overeating
  • Consider your digestive fire (Agni) when choosing foods

Ayurvedic Lifestyle Recommendations

  • Follow a daily routine (Dinacharya) aligned with natural cycles
  • Wake up early, preferably before sunrise
  • Practice oil pulling and tongue scraping for oral health
  • Take regular oil massages (Abhyanga) for body nourishment
  • Avoid suppressing natural urges (Vegas)
  • Balance work, rest, and recreational activities
  • Spend time in nature and get adequate sunlight
  • Maintain emotional balance through meditation and counseling
  • Follow seasonal regimens (Ritucharya) for optimal health

Core Ayurvedic Principles

  • Understand your unique constitution (Prakriti) and current state (Vikriti)
  • Balance the three doshas: Vata, Pitta, and Kapha
  • Strengthen your digestive fire (Agni) for proper metabolism
  • Support natural detoxification through proper elimination
  • Use herbs and treatments specific to your individual needs
  • Practice Panchakarma for deep cleansing and rejuvenation
  • Maintain mental clarity through Satvavajaya (psychotherapy)
  • Follow ethical conduct (Achar Rasayana) for holistic wellness
  • Seek balance in all aspects of life - physical, mental, and spiritual

Yoga & Wellness Practices

  • Practice daily yoga asanas appropriate for your body type
  • Include pranayama (breathing exercises) in your routine
  • Start with simple poses and gradually advance
  • Practice meditation for mental clarity and peace
  • Use yoga therapy for specific health conditions
  • Combine physical poses with mindful breathing
  • Practice yoga nidra for deep relaxation
  • Include dharana (concentration) and dhyana (meditation)
  • Follow the eight limbs of yoga for complete wellness

Disease Prevention & Health Maintenance

  • Get regular Ayurvedic consultations and pulse diagnosis
  • Follow preventive Panchakarma treatments seasonally
  • Maintain proper hygiene and cleanliness
  • Build immunity through Rasayana (rejuvenative) therapies
  • Address imbalances early before they manifest as disease
  • Practice stress management through yoga and meditation
  • Maintain healthy relationships and social connections
  • Stay informed about Ayurvedic principles and self-care
  • Seek guidance for constitutional analysis and personalized care
